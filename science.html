These are the science stream career options after 10th! Science stream is a very common choice among 10th standard passed students in India. Students who want to pursue applied and pure science professional courses in the future may choose this stream. There are a variety of boards in India, subjects to study, professional courses available, prospects, and science stream career options after 10th which can be chosen according to each one’s convenience.


The Science stream is a bit challenging when compared to all the other main streams like Arts and Commerce. Academically talented students won’t have much difficulty in facing the subjects present in this stream.


Science Stream Boards Available In India

There are four important education boards available in India. Students can choose a stream that suits them the best. The choice should be made based on the basis of the aptitude of the student and the difficulty level associated with the boards.


CBSE (Central Board of Secondary Education)
ICSE (Indian Certificate of Secondary Education)
IB (International Baccalaureate) State Boards

IB is the hardest board among the above mentioned ones. It is laid on the ‘practical aspects’ and ‘application knowledge’ of learning.


When it comes to the level of difficulty, IB is followed by the ICSE board. Then comes the CBSE board which is almost known by everyone and it functions under the Union Government of India. The level of difficulty associated with this board is moderate.


Finally, comes the State Boards which function under the respective state governments of each state. The medium of instruction in the case of schools affiliated with such boards is English and regional language.


Science Stream Groups

There are two main groups within it when it comes to the science stream. They are -


Mathematics Group (Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics as main subjects)
Biology Group (Biology, Physics, and Chemistry as main subjects)

These groups end up determining the career path of a student to an extent. A student may pursue both of them at the same time or may choose any of the above mentioned groups. The choice can be taken according to each one’s abilities.


The biology group is generally known as the B group and the mathematics group is generally known as the A group. Students pursuing both groups may assume themselves as AB group students.


Science Stream Subjects
  1. Physics
  2. Chemistry
  3. Biology
  4. English
  5. Computer Science
  6. Mathematics

Among the above mentioned ones, Mathematics/Biology, Physics, Chemistry, and English are the mandatory subjects. Students have to choose the optional ones other than them. Usually, the availability of such optional subjects may vary from one board to the other and from one state board to the other. Those subjects are Sanskrit, Computer Science, regional languages, etc.


1) Physics

Physics is the oldest among academic fields in the world and is the science of motion, matter, energy, and force.

Examples of a few topics which covers are:

  • Kinematics
  • Laws of Motion
  • Electrostatics
  • Thermodynamics
  • Electronic Devices
  • Electromagnetic Waves

2) Chemistry

The study of Chemistry is based on the properties, composition, the structure of matter, including atomic structure and the changes influenced by the chemical reactions.


Examples of a few topics which covers are:

  • Chemical Thermodynamics
  • Organic Chemistry
  • Chemical Kinetics
  • Surface Chemistry
  • Molecular Structure and Chemical Bonding

3) Biology

Biology deals with living organisms such as animals and plants including facets such as their physiological mechanisms, physical structures, chemical processes, and development.


Examples of a few topics which covers are:


  • Structural Organisation in Animals and Plants
  • Human Physiology
  • Plant Physiology
  • Genetics and Evolution
  • Biotechnology and its applications
4) Mathematics

Mathematics focuses on the concepts of change, quantity, space and structure.


Examples of a few topics which covers are:

  • Algebra
  • Calculus
  • Mathematical Reasoning
  • Probability
  • Linear Programming

Evaluation and Academic Program

There are two academic years present which are 12th and 11th standards. In the case of state boards and the other boards, each academic year gets divided into two semesters with each one lasting for a period of 6 months. Main evaluation exams are conducted at the end of each academic year in the case of a semester learning system.
